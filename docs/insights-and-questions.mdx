Insights are reusable questions that help your team evaluate opportunities consistently.

## What makes a good insight

A strong insight asks for something the source material can reasonably answer.

Good examples:

- `Does this opportunity require prior federal past performance?`
- `What certifications or licenses are required?`
- `Is this likely to need on-site staffing?`
- `What are the main evaluation criteria?`
- `Does the solicitation mention incumbent contractors?`

Weak examples:

- `Should we bid?`
- `Will we win?`
- `Is this good?`

Those questions depend on internal strategy, pricing, relationships, and capacity. Use AcornBids to extract evidence, then let your team make the pursuit decision.

## Review answers

When reviewing generated answers, check:

- Whether the answer cites the right source material
- Whether the answer is specific enough to support a decision
- Whether missing information is called out instead of guessed
- Whether the same question should be reused across a saved search

## Suggested starting questions

Start with a small set:

- Eligibility requirements
- Due date and submission requirements
- Contract vehicle or award type
- Required past performance
- Place of performance
- Evaluation criteria
- Required documents or attachments
