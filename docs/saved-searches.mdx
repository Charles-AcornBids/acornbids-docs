Saved searches let your team turn one-off filtering into repeatable market coverage.

## When to create a saved search

Create a saved search when you want to monitor:

- A customer or agency
- A capability area
- A contract vehicle or program
- A geography
- A set-aside category
- A keyword pattern your team qualifies often

## Build a useful saved search

1. Start broad enough to avoid missing relevant opportunities.
2. Add exclusion terms only when the results are consistently noisy.
3. Use date and opportunity-type filters for the review cadence your team actually follows.
4. Add insights after the saved search is stable.
5. Turn on alerts only after the search quality is acceptable.

## Naming pattern

Use names that make ownership and intent clear:

```text
[Customer or market] - [Capability] - [Review cadence]
```

Examples:

- `DHS - Cybersecurity - Weekly`
- `Louisiana - Workforce Grants - Daily`
- `DoD - Facilities Maintenance - Capture Review`
